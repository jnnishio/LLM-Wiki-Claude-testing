# Veltrix Research Consortium (VRC)

The Veltrix Research Consortium is the parent organisation governing all VRC research programs, facilities, instrumentation, teams, and personnel documented in this wiki. It operates as a consortium-funded scientific body focused on polar, atmospheric, and environmental research at high-latitude sites.

## Leadership

- **Director of Research:** [[tarquin-selvidge]] (since 2017)
- The VRC Science Committee reviews and approves major program changes (e.g., NOVA's duration extension from 720 to 847 days, VRC-SC-MIN-2022-09).

- [[oswick-station]] (FAC-OSW-001) — northern polar anchor site, 74°N
- [[halvern-testing-ground]] (FAC-HLV-002) — primary inland test and operations base, 68°N
- Crestfield Lab (FAC-CRF-003) — HELIOS ground station
- Thule Relay Outpost (FAC-TRL-007) — HELIOS and SABLE remote site

## Cross-Program Infrastructure

- **VRC Data Processing Centre (VDPC):** Located at Halvern; processes VBIF v2 format data from NOVA, HELIOS, AETHER
- **VRC Integrated Atmosphere Archive (VIAA):** Receives quarterly merged datasets from NOVA, AETHER, and HELIOS
- **VBIF v2 (VRC Binary Interchange Format):** Common data format across programs
- **Irixon-4 telemetry backbone:** Carries sensor data from field nodes to VDPC

## Safety and Compliance

- Safety standard: VSS-2019-04 (applies to all field activities)
- Emergency Operations Centre: ext. 7-700
- Cryospheric sampling: governed by [[clearwater-protocol]]
- Data anomaly management: governed by [[duskline-protocol]]
