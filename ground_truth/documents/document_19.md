## 1. Mission and Mandate

Team Crestfield is the Veltrix Research Consortium's instrumentation and sensor engineering team, responsible for the design, build, calibration, and lifecycle maintenance of VRC scientific instruments. The team holds technical authority over all sensors and instruments used in VRC research programs.

The team's principal deliverables are:

- Sensor system development and specification documentation (system codes SYS-*)
- Factory and field calibration of all VRC instruments
- Technical authority review for all instrument deployments
- Liaison with external calibration facilities and standards bodies

Team Crestfield is the issuing authority for the **Kryon-7** (Document 02), **Kryon-7X** (Document 18), **Peltex-200** (Documents 13 and 14), and **Valdyne Spectral Array** (Document 23) specifications.

## 3. Primary Responsibilities

### 3.1 Technical Authority

Dr. Renna Holt is the VRC Technical Authority for all scientific sensors and instruments. This role requires:

- Review and sign-off on all new instrument deployments
- Approval of any deviation from published instrument specifications
- Final arbitration on calibration disputes between programs

### 3.3 Calibration Services

Kofi Twumasi-Mensah is the sole designated calibration specialist for the Peltex-200 and Kryon-7X. All calibration activities must be coordinated through him. Calibration certificates are issued under VRC-CAL-* codes and archived in the VRC Calibration Registry (VRC-CAL-REG-LIVE).

The Team Crestfield calibration laboratory is located at Halvern Testing Ground (HLV-B03, ISO 7 clean room; see Document 16).

## 4. Facilities

Team Crestfield operates from two locations:

- **Primary:** Calibration laboratory HLV-B03, Halvern Testing Ground (Document 16) — full calibration and sensor assembly capability
- **Secondary:** Workshop bay HLV-B02-EAST, adjacent to Atlas hangar — field repair and assembly

Field calibration support is provided at Oswick Station (Document 03) through periodic visits (typically quarterly).
