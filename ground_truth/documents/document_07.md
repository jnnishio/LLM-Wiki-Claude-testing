## 1. Mission and Mandate

Team Velanthor is one of the primary research teams within the Veltrix Research Consortium (VRC), specialising in atmospheric science, field instrumentation, and high-latitude environmental monitoring. The team was chartered on 01 September 2019 under the leadership of Dr. Mara Fenwick following the reorganisation of the former VRC Atmospheric Sciences Division.

The team's mandate encompasses:

- Designing and executing atmospheric measurement campaigns
- Operating and maintaining VRC sensor infrastructure at high-latitude sites
- Developing data processing algorithms for aerosol and trace gas retrievals
- Providing scientific expertise for cross-program collaborations

Team Velanthor is the primary operating team for **Project NOVA** (Document 01) and contributes to **Project HELIOS** (Document 04) and **Project AETHER** (Document 17).

## 3. Primary Responsibilities

### 3.1 Project NOVA (VRC-ATM-2201)

Team Velanthor holds primary ownership of Project NOVA (Document 01). The team is responsible for:

- Operation and maintenance of all 14 NOVA sensor nodes
- Data receipt, validation, and archiving at VDPC (Halvern)
- Atmospheric retrieval algorithm development and maintenance
- Annual reporting to the VRC Science Committee

### 3.2 Project HELIOS (VRC-SOL-1887)

Dr. Osias Prewitt leads HELIOS (Document 04) from within Team Velanthor. Dr. Mara Fenwick serves as Deputy PI.

### 3.3 Atlas Platform Support

Team Velanthor is the primary operational user of the **Atlas-X** autonomous platform (Document 09) for NOVA Zone C deployments. Platform maintenance is coordinated with Team Crestfield per Document 19, Section 4.

### 3.4 Duskline Protocol Compliance

All Team Velanthor field operations must comply with the **Duskline Protocol** (Document 20). Dafydd Emlyn is the designated Duskline Protocol compliance officer for the team.
