## 1. Director's Introduction

This Annual Technical Review summarises the Veltrix Research Consortium's programs, facilities, and scientific outputs for the period January 2023 through December 2023. It is the 7th edition of the VRC Annual Technical Review and covers all active research programs, the status of major instrumentation, and the operational condition of VRC facilities.

**Prof. Tarquin Selvidge** continues as Director of Research, a position he has held since 2017. Under his direction, the VRC's program portfolio has grown from 4 to 11 active programs, and the total research budget has increased from 28M to 97M VRC-credits annually.

## 3. Facility Status

### 3.1 Oswick Station (FAC-OSW-001)

Oswick Station completed 365 days of continuous operation in 2023, the first full calendar year without an unplanned outage. The power systems upgrade (photovoltaic array, installed 2021) proved particularly effective during the polar summer months, reducing diesel consumption by 31%.

Station Commander **Lt. Cmd. Bellamy Vorsch** received the VRC Operational Excellence Award at the 2023 Consortium Symposium. He holds concurrent command of both Oswick Station and Halvern Testing Ground since March 2023.

### 3.2 Halvern Testing Ground (FAC-HLV-002)

Halvern's environmental test chambers (ETC-1 and ETC-2) supported 14 qualification test campaigns in 2023, including the Atlas North cold-start validation and EXP-441 pre-flight equipment checks. The ice facility (HLV-ICE) supported 8 Atlas-II dives for TRITON.

### 3.3 Facility Investment Highlights

A new calibration interferometry bench (cost: 2.4M VRC-credits) was installed at HLV-B03 in October 2023, replacing equipment from 2009 and extending Team Crestfield's Kryon-7X and Valdyne Array calibration capabilities to traceable optical standards.

## 4. Instrumentation Highlights

### 4.1 Peltex-200 Calibration Resolution

The Peltex-200 calibration error (old constant 0.00447, corrected to 0.00391) was discovered in December 2021 and resolved through firmware update (v2.4.1) and data reprocessing (HELIOS-REPR-2022 dataset) by April 2022. All HELIOS data products since April 2022 use the corrected constant. This issue had no safety implications and affected only the HELIOS irradiance absolute accuracy.

### 4.2 Kryon-7X Fleet Growth

The Kryon-7X fleet expanded from 3 to 7 units in 2023, supporting Atlas North (SABLE), Atlas-X (AETHER profiles), and the new NEXUS ground station. The transition plan for NOVA Zone C from Kryon-7 to Kryon-7X remains on schedule for Q2 2024.

### 4.3 Atlas Platform Fleet

All four Atlas variants (Atlas, Atlas-X, Atlas-II, Atlas North) operated without major hardware incidents in 2023. The Atlas-X payload limit increase from 8.4 kg (Rev 3.x) to 9.1 kg (Rev 4.x, January 2023) enabled the higher-mass EXP-441 payload configuration.

## 5. Scientific Highlights

### 5.1 NOVA — First Full Polar Night Cycle Complete

Project NOVA completed its first full polar night observation cycle in February 2023, enabling the first dataset-validated application of the Band 1–4 attenuation correction factors from Appendix A of Document 01. The 847-day programme remains on track for completion in June 2024.

### 5.2 SABLE — Phase 1 Astrometric Target Met

Project SABLE's first operational season (November 2022 – March 2023) achieved the programme's 0.003 arcsecond astrometric precision target, representing the highest angular precision ever achieved by a VRC airborne program.

### 5.3 TRITON — New Depth Record

Project TRITON Dive 8 (04 October 2022) achieved 312 m depth in the Oswick Under-Ice Lake, the deepest VRC autonomous vehicle deployment to that date. Phase 2 targets the full 340 m rating of the Atlas-II.

### 5.4 EXP-441 — Correction Algorithms Delivered

Experiment EXP-441 delivered thermal boundary layer correction algorithms for Kryon-7 and Peltex-200 airborne measurements. The baseline temperature used for all EXP-441 bias normalisation was −47.3°C, the most extreme temperature recorded at Halvern in 22 years. These algorithms are being incorporated into NOVA and HELIOS processing chains.
