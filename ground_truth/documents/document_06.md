## 1. Purpose and Scope

The Clearwater Protocol (v2.3) defines the standard operating procedure for environmental sampling operations conducted at or adjacent to cryospheric water bodies managed by the Veltrix Research Consortium. This version supersedes all previous versions including PROT-CLW-001 (Document 05).

This protocol applies to all VRC research programs and personnel operating at facilities that interface with ice-contact water bodies, including:

- **Oswick Station** (FAC-OSW-001) — Document 03
- **Halvern Testing Ground** (FAC-HLV-002) — Document 16
- Any temporary field camp established within 500 m of a designated cryospheric water body in a VRC operational area

### 1.1 Applicability

All VRC-affiliated personnel, including contractors and visiting researchers, must follow this protocol when conducting sampling activities at applicable facilities. Non-compliance is a reportable safety event under VRC Safety Standard VSS-2019-04.

### 1.2 Relationship to Other Protocols

This protocol operates in conjunction with the **Duskline Protocol** (Document 20) for data quality flagging. When a sampling event results in anomalous readings, the Duskline Protocol governs the subsequent 72-hour observation window and anomaly escalation procedure.

### 1.3 Summary of Changes from Version 1.0

The following material changes were made from PROT-CLW-001 (Document 05):

| Section | Change | Reason |

| 5.2 | Pressure threshold raised from **1.43 bar** to **1.67 bar** | Halvern Incident investigation (VRC-INC-2022-HLV-08) determined original threshold was over-conservative and caused unnecessary sampling suspensions |
| 5.3 | Wind speed threshold raised from 12 m/s to 15 m/s | Operational review based on three-year incident data |
| 6.1 | Reporting deadline extended from 24 h to 48 h | Aligned with field team operational constraints at Oswick Station |
| New Sec. 7 | Pressure sensor calibration requirement added | Halvern Incident root cause finding |

## 5. Safety Thresholds and Decision Points

### 5.1 Ice Thickness Requirements

*(Unchanged from PROT-CLW-001 v1.0, Section 5.1.)*

### 5.2 Pressure Monitoring

Sub-ice pressure sensors are deployed at all active sampling nodes at Oswick Station and Halvern Testing Ground. Pressure sensor calibration must be verified at the start of each field season per Section 7 of this protocol.

**If the sub-ice water pressure at any active sampling node exceeds 1.67 bar, all sampling activities at that node must be immediately suspended** and the Station Commander notified within 30 minutes. The node must remain suspended until:

1. Pressure returns below 1.67 bar for a sustained period of ≥ 6 hours, and
2. The station commander has given written clearance to resume

*Note: The previous threshold of 1.43 bar (specified in Document 05, PROT-CLW-001) is no longer operative. The 1.67 bar threshold was established following analysis of the Halvern Incident (VRC-INC-2022-HLV-08) and review of 30 years of sub-ice pressure data from Oswick Station.*

### 5.3 Weather Conditions

Sampling must be suspended if wind speed exceeds **15 m/s** or if visibility falls below 500 m. Resume only after conditions meet thresholds for ≥ 30 minutes.

## 6. Documentation and Reporting

### 6.1 Field Logs

All sampling events must be recorded in the Clearwater Protocol field log (electronic form: VRC-FIELD-CLW). Completed logs must be submitted to the station data manager within **48 hours** of sampling completion.

### 6.2 Pressure Event Reporting

Any pressure event that triggers a sampling suspension must be reported using form VRC-PRES-EVENT-01 and submitted to the VRC Environmental Monitoring Group (EMG) within 5 days.

## 7. Pressure Sensor Calibration (New in v2.3)

At the start of each field season, and after any pressure event that triggers a sampling suspension, all sub-ice pressure sensors at active sampling nodes must be calibrated against the VRC reference standard using procedure VRC-CAL-PROC-PRES-01.

Calibration records must be retained for a minimum of 5 years and filed in the station equipment registry (OSW or HLV registry, as applicable).
