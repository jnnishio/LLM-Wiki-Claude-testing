## 1. Purpose and Scope

The Clearwater Protocol defines the standard operating procedure for environmental sampling operations conducted at or adjacent to cryospheric water bodies managed by the Veltrix Research Consortium. It governs sample collection, in-situ measurement, and chain-of-custody handling for water chemistry, suspended particulate, and dissolved gas analyses.

This protocol applies to all VRC research programs operating at facilities that interface with ice-contact water bodies. Primary applicable facilities as of the 2020 issue date include:

- Oswick Station (FAC-OSW-001) — northern polar lake sampling
- Halvern Testing Ground (FAC-HLV-002) — glacial meltwater monitoring

The protocol does not apply to marine or open-ocean sampling; for those contexts, refer to VRC Protocol PROT-MAR-004.

## 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Cryospheric water body | Any naturally occurring liquid or partially frozen water mass in contact with glacial ice or permafrost within a VRC operational area |
| Sampling node | A designated water column access point established per Section 3.1 |
| Reference sample | A clean-handled replicate collected simultaneously with every field sample for laboratory QC |
| Chain-of-custody form | Form VRC-COC-WTR-01 |

## 3. Sampling Setup

### 3.1 Establishing a Sampling Node

A sampling node must be established at each designated measurement site prior to any sampling activity. The node establishment procedure:

1. Survey and mark the sampling point with a VRC-standard marker buoy (model OSW-BUY-01 or equivalent)
2. Record GPS coordinates to ±3 m accuracy using the VRC-GNSS-001 receiver or equivalent
3. Measure and record water depth profile to 0.5 m intervals
4. Install the sample collection winch system (part code VRC-WINCH-CRYO-02) per the manufacturer's procedure

### 3.2 Equipment Checklist

Before commencing sampling, the field team lead must confirm all of the following:

- [ ] Chain-of-custody forms (VRC-COC-WTR-01) pre-filled with site details
- [ ] Sample bottles (amber glass, 250 mL, pre-cleaned to VRC-BOTTLE-CRYO standard)
- [ ] Depth profiler (Halvdyne DP-110 or approved equivalent)
- [ ] In-situ CTD probe (approved model list: HLV-CTD-LIST-2019)
- [ ] Personal cold-water safety kit (VRC-SAFE-CWATER-01)

## 4. Measurement Procedure

### 4.1 In-Situ Parameters

At each sampling node, record the following in-situ parameters before collecting any water samples:

- Water temperature (at surface, 1 m, 5 m depth intervals)
- Specific conductance
- Dissolved oxygen (mg/L and % saturation)
- pH
- Turbidity (NTU)

### 4.2 Sample Collection

Collect samples using the CRYO-COLLECT procedure:

1. Lower the sampler to the target depth at ≤ 0.5 m/s descent rate
2. Trigger collection when depth indicator stabilises (±0.1 m)
3. Retrieve sampler at ≤ 0.3 m/s ascent rate to prevent sample disturbance
4. Transfer sample to pre-labelled bottles within 5 minutes of retrieval
5. Complete and sign the chain-of-custody form immediately

## 5. Safety Thresholds and Decision Points

### 5.1 Ice Thickness Requirements

Sampling on or adjacent to ice-covered water bodies requires minimum ice thickness verification:

| Activity | Minimum Ice Thickness |
|----------|----------------------|
| Walking access (≤ 75 kg person) | 15 cm |
| Light equipment (≤ 200 kg) | 30 cm |
| Tracked vehicle (≤ 2,000 kg) | 70 cm |

### 5.2 Pressure Monitoring

At Oswick Station and Halvern, sub-ice pressure sensors are deployed at all active sampling nodes. **If the sub-ice water pressure at any node exceeds 1.43 bar, all sampling activities at that node must be suspended immediately** and the Station Commander notified.

*Note for historical record: This pressure threshold was revised in Clearwater Protocol v2.3 (Document 06) following the Halvern Incident of August 2022 (VRC-INC-2022-HLV-08). The current threshold is 1.67 bar. Refer to Document 06 for all active operations.*

### 5.3 Weather Conditions

Sampling must be suspended if wind speed exceeds 12 m/s or if visibility falls below 500 m. Resume only after conditions return to threshold levels for a sustained period of at least 30 minutes.

## 6. Documentation and Reporting

All sampling events must be recorded in the Clearwater Protocol field log (electronic form: VRC-FIELD-CLW). Completed logs must be submitted to the station data manager within 24 hours of sampling completion.
