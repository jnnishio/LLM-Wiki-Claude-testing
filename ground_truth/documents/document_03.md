## 1. Facility Overview

Oswick Station is a permanent research and monitoring facility operated by the Veltrix Research Consortium (VRC). Established in 1997, the station serves as the primary northern anchor point for polar-environment research programs including **Project NOVA** (Document 01), the **Clearwater Protocol** operations (Documents 05 and 06), and **Experiment EXP-119** (Document 21).

The station is staffed year-round and provides infrastructure for sensor deployment, data relay, personnel accommodation, and emergency response for all VRC activities in the northern polar region.

### 3.2 Power Systems

Oswick Station is powered by dual Halvdyne Series-9 diesel generators (primary 85 kW, backup 60 kW) supplemented by a 40 kW rooftop photovoltaic array (installed March 2021). Total continuous power budget is 125 kW. Generator fuel resupply is scheduled every 60 days.

### 3.3 Communications

The station operates a satellite communications system providing:
- 50 Mbit/s downlink / 20 Mbit/s uplink (Ka-band, Veltrix SatNet)
- VRC private network tunnel (VPN code: OSW-VPN-TUN-07)
- Irixon-4 telemetry relay for all NOVA and AETHER sensor nodes in Zone A

## 4. Safety Protocols

### 4.1 Emergency Procedures

Emergency mustering point: **OSW-EMP-01** (north face of OSW-B01, clearly marked).  
Emergency frequency: 156.8 MHz (VRC channel 16).  
All incidents must be reported within 4 hours to Station Commander and to VRC Emergency Operations Centre (ext. 7-700).

### 4.2 Hazardous Environment Protocols

Personnel conducting outdoor work when ambient temperature is below −35°C must follow **Protocol OSW-COLD-01**, which requires:

- Minimum two-person teams for all outdoor tasks
- Hourly radio check-in with Station Control
- Full cold-weather personal protective equipment (PPE set: VRC-PPE-POLAR-02)
- Maximum continuous outdoor exposure: 45 minutes before mandatory warm-up break

Personnel conducting work on the OSW-T01 sensor mast must be certified under VRC-CERT-HEIGHT-03. Mast work is prohibited when wind speed exceeds 14 m/s.

### 4.3 Chemical Storage

Laboratory chemicals are stored per VRC Hazmat Code VHC-007. The station maintains a chemical inventory updated monthly; current inventory is held in the OSW Laboratory Management System (OSLAB-INV-LIVE).
